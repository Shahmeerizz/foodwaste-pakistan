01
Farm — Punjab fields
Harvest losses
−10 to 15%
No protective crates. Produce is thrown directly into sacks and stacked. Fragile items like tomatoes are bruised before the truck even starts moving. No packaging standards, no quality control at source.
02
Transit — road to Lahore
Open-truck damage
−15 to 20%
Open tractor-trolleys with zero refrigeration on roads that can take 6–10 hours. 40°C+ heat accelerates spoilage. Excessive stacking crushes lower layers. By arrival, 1 in 5 items is already unsellable.
03
Sabzi Mandi, Lahore
Floor storage & load-shedding
−20 to 25%
No cold storage exists anywhere in the market. Produce goes directly onto bare concrete floors. Load-shedding — up to 20 hours in some areas — destroys overnight stocks even for vendors who own refrigerators. We documented tonnes of edible vegetables abandoned at market close every single evening.
04
Barkat Market & retail
The freshness bias
−5 to 10%
Our survey found 66% of Pakistanis refuse to buy slightly bruised or "ugly" vegetables even at a discounted price. Perfectly nutritious produce is rejected purely on cosmetic grounds. Retailers have no markdown system — unsold stock goes straight to waste.
05
Lakhodair Landfill, Lahore
Final destination — the dumpster
UP TO 40% TOTAL
Once food is mixed with plastic waste on market floors, it is no longer rescuable. It goes to landfill where it decomposes anaerobically, releasing methane. No biogas capture. No protective liner. Leachate seeps directly into groundwater. The soil toxicity near Lakhodair is 3× the safe agricultural limit.

Root Causes

Why the system
keeps failing

⚡
Energy crisis
18–20 hours of load-shedding in rural areas makes refrigeration functionally useless for most of the day. Every cold storage unit becomes an expensive shelf.
🚛
No cold chain
Fewer than 5% of fresh produce travels in refrigerated vehicles. Open tractor-trolleys are the norm from farm to market — across hundreds of kilometres in peak summer.
⚖️
Zero legislation
Pakistan has no national food waste law. The 2000 wedding restriction law exists on paper and has never once been enforced. Donors have no legal protection.
🎪
Wedding culture
Multi-dish weddings are a performance of wealth and generosity. 78% of our survey respondents rated wedding waste as extreme. The food street industry faces the same pressure nightly.
👁️
Freshness bias
66% of Pakistanis won't buy slightly bruised produce even at a discount. This cosmetic standard condemns thousands of tonnes of nutritious food every week.
📊
No data systems
Pakistan has no national food waste monitoring mechanism. Without measurement, policy cannot be designed or evaluated. What isn't counted doesn't get fixed.
Our Proposal

The Rescue &
Regenerate Model

A two-tier framework designed for Pakistan's specific context — one tier for edible food, one tier for spoiled food. Nothing goes to waste.

Tier 1
Rescue
Edible food → human use
Food Rescue App
Restaurant managers alert NGOs (Rizq, Saylani) of surplus between 1–3 AM via a digital platform — before food is discarded.
Mandi Rescue Desks
Physical drop-off points at Sabzi Mandis for B-grade produce. Nutritious but cosmetically imperfect food redirected to Dastarkhwans and community kitchens.
Good Samaritan Law
Legal protection for food donors — modelled on the USA's Bill Emerson Act (1996). Removes the single biggest psychological barrier to donation.
Tax credits for donors
Businesses redirecting 20%+ of surplus food to charities receive tax incentives. Makes civic responsibility financially rational.
Tier 2
Regenerate
Spoiled food → useful resource
Composting units at Mandis
Decentralised composting converts spoiled produce into fertiliser — sold back to the same farmers who supply the market. A closed loop.
Biogas digesters
Organic waste from markets like Badami Bagh produces biogas that powers the cold storage units those markets so desperately need. Waste generates the solution.
Solar cold storage (PPP)
Interest-free public-private loans for solar-powered cold storage at Sabzi Mandis. Directly defeats load-shedding as a root cause.
Green bin mandate
LWMC and SSWMB enforce organic-only bins at every market vendor. Prevents plastic contamination that currently makes food rescue impossible.
